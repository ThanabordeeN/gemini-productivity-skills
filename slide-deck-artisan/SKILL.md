These instructions guide the creation of presentation slides.
Output .pptx files, generated via code (e.g., using Node.js PptxGenJS or similar libraries).

THE ESSENTIAL PRINCIPLE
You receive a Design Philosophy (.md file) and contextual content. Your job is to EXPRESS IT VISUALLY across a sequence of 16:9 presentation slides.

The philosophy provides the aesthetic language, and you must translate that into layout, typography, whitespace, and structural composition within a .pptx format.

DEDUCING THE SUBTLE REFERENCE
CRITICAL STEP: Before generating the presentation, identify the subtle conceptual thread from the original user request.
The topic is a nuanced reference embedded within the deck itself. Someone familiar with the subject should feel it intuitively through your pacing and structural choices. The design philosophy provides the aesthetic language; the deduced topic provides the conceptual DNA woven invisibly into form, structure, and composition.

DECK CREATION PROCESS

1. Clear the Mind: Review the design philosophy. Anchor your creative approach in its principles.
2. Visual Space > Text: A presentation is not a document. Ensure text is sparse, clinical, and essential-only. Never use paragraphs; use massive intentional typography or microscopic, precise annotations.
3. Pacing and Rhythm: Treat the sequence of slides like movements in a symphony. Allow concepts to breathe across multiple slides. Build meaning through patient repetition.
4. Information Architecture: Borrow the visual language of systematic observation where appropriate—strict grids, clinical alignments, layered structural hierarchies.
5. Expert Craftsmanship: To achieve human-crafted quality (not AI-generated), create slides that look like they took countless hours. Make it appear as though someone at the absolute top of their presentation design field labored over every single alignment, negative space, and font choice. Double check that nothing overlaps and formatting is flawless.
6. Execution via Code: Write scripts to generate the presentation. Ensure your code perfectly reflects the aesthetic requirements—exact font sizes, strict positional coordinates, and cohesive color hex codes. Let context guide whether that means whisper-quiet labels or bold typographic gestures.

REFINEMENT - FINAL STEP

IMPORTANT: The user ALREADY said "It isn't perfect enough. It must be pristine, a masterpiece of craftsmanship, as if it were about to be displayed in a museum."

CRITICAL: To refine the work before finalizing the .pptx generation, review your coded layouts. Avoid adding "more graphics" just to fill space. Instead, refine what has been created to make it extremely crisp. Respect the design philosophy and the power of negative space entirely. If the instinct is to clutter the slide with more charts or icons, STOP, and ask: "How can I make the existing layout more of an architectural masterpiece?"

Take a second pass over your generation code. Adjust coordinates, align margins precisely, refine the color palette, and polish further to make this a philosophically designed, undeniable masterpiece.

Output the final result as a single, downloadable .pptx file, alongside the design philosophy used as a .md file.
