These are instructions for creating design philosophies - aesthetic movements that are then EXPRESSED VISUALLY in presentations. Output only .md files.

Complete this in the first step of presentation creation:
DESIGN PHILOSOPHY CREATION

To begin, create a VISUAL PHILOSOPHY (not layouts or templates) that will be interpreted through:

- Form, space, color, pacing across screens
- Graphics, structural grids, data visualization
- Minimal text as a primary visual anchor

THE CRITICAL UNDERSTANDING

- What is received: Some subtle input or instructions by the user that should be taken into account, but used as a foundation; it should not constrain creative freedom.
- What is created: A design philosophy/aesthetic movement tailored for slide presentations.
- What happens next: Then, the next skill receives the philosophy and EXPRESSES IT VISUALLY - creating a presentation that is 90% visual storytelling, 10% essential text.

Consider this approach:

- Write a manifesto for a presentation aesthetic movement.

The philosophy must emphasize: Visual expression. Spatial communication across 16:9 screens. Narrative pacing. Minimal words.

HOW TO GENERATE A VISUAL PHILOSOPHY

Name the movement (1-2 words): "Structural Void" / "Kinetic Authority" / "Information Brutalism"

Articulate the philosophy (4-6 paragraphs - concise but complete):

To capture the VISUAL essence, express how the philosophy manifests through:

- Hierarchy and typographic scale (e.g., massive headlines vs. tiny data labels)
- Slide pacing and rhythm
- Color theory and material depth (e.g., glassmorphism, flat vectors)
- Grid structures and negative space within the 16:9 format
- Data visualization techniques

CRITICAL GUIDELINES:

- Avoid redundancy: Each design aspect should be mentioned once.
- Emphasize craftsmanship REPEATEDLY: The philosophy MUST stress multiple times that the final presentation should appear as though it took countless hours to create, was labored over with care, and comes from someone at the absolute top of their presentation design field. Use phrases like "painstaking attention to alignment," "master-level visual hierarchy," "meticulously crafted data," "expert spatial orchestration."
- Leave creative space: Remain specific about the aesthetic direction, but concise enough that the presentation creator has room to make interpretive, high-level structural choices per slide.

The philosophy must guide the next version to express ideas VISUALLY, not through text. Information lives in design, structure, and pacing, not bullet points.

PHILOSOPHY EXAMPLES

"Data Minimalism" Philosophy: Communication through monumental data points and bold simplicity. Visual expression: Massive color blocks, sculptural typography (huge single metrics, tiny contextual labels), Brutalist spatial divisions across slides. Ideas expressed through visual weight and scale, not explanation. Text as rare, powerful gesture - never paragraphs or standard bullet points. Every chart element placed with the precision of a master craftsman.

"Cinematic Space" Philosophy: Quiet visual contemplation through expansive negative space and narrative focus. Visual expression: Edge-to-edge photography, deep atmospheric backgrounds, vast negative space holding single insights. Typography whispered (small, refined, serving the visual). Images breathe across screens. Text appears sparingly - short phrases, never explanatory blocks. Each slide balanced with the care of a cinematic director.

"Structural Grids" Philosophy: Pure order and restraint framing complex ideas. Visual expression: Strict grid-based precision, high contrast, dramatic empty zones. Typography precise but modular - aligning perfectly with the invisible architecture. Structure communicates authority, not words. Every alignment the work of countless, meticulous refinements by top-tier designers.

ESSENTIAL PRINCIPLES

- VISUAL PHILOSOPHY: Create an aesthetic worldview to be expressed through presentation design.
- MINIMAL TEXT: Always emphasize that presentation text is sparse, essential-only, integrated as visual element - never lengthy bullet lists.
- SPATIAL EXPRESSION: Ideas communicate through pacing, layout, scale, and composition - not paragraphs.
- ARTISTIC FREEDOM: The deck creator interprets the philosophy visually - provide creative room.
- EXPERT CRAFTSMANSHIP: Repeatedly emphasize the final presentation must look meticulously crafted, labored over with care, the product of countless hours by someone at the top of their field.

Output the design philosophy as a .md file. Avoid mentioning the specific intention of the presentation, keeping the design philosophy generic enough to be applied universally.
